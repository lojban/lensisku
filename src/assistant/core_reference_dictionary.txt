## learn-lojban tutorial (notions; English ↔ English, from lojban.pw course themes)

Alphabet ↔ Latin letters; ' = /h/ between vowels; . = pause before vowel-initial words; stress on the second-to-last vowel.
selbrivla (brivla) ↔ Relation/content word: consonant cluster within first five sounds, ends in a vowel (e.g. gleki, klama).
cmavo ↔ Particle: consonant + vowel (+ optional 'V sequences); may be run together (e.g. lenu, naku).
cmevla ↔ Name word: ends in a consonant; often written .name. with pauses matching dots.
Bridi ↔ A clause: sumti fill numbered places x₁ x₂ … of one selbri (relation).
cu ↔ Separates sumti cluster from selbri when needed; not used before nu/du'u/ka abstractions as selbri.
fa fe fi fo fu (place tags) ↔ Tag sumti to selbri places x₁–x₅; reorder sumti freely; unlike SE conversion they do not reshuffle the place structure; after be they mark places inside a tanru sumti (e.g. le klama be fi le tcadu).
Modal terms (tense, location, cause, etc.) ↔ Apply to the whole bridi; they do not fill numbered selbri slots—omitted slots are still zo'e unless a place tag assigns them (learn-lojban: ca, fa'a, pu, bu'u, …).
Modal term without a following sumti ↔ Locates the event relative to the speaker's here and now (e.g. ba alone = future from now; bu'u alone = at this place).
Modal term with a following sumti ↔ Locates the event relative to the event or state in that argument (e.g. ba le nu mi cadzu = after I walk).
Time and space (same principle) ↔ Temporal distance uses pu/ba with zi/za/zu; spatial distance uses vi/va/vu (short / medium / far); vowel order i–a–u repeats for subjective near/medium/long.
la'u ↔ Modal with a sumti stating how far in time or space (e.g. in three days).
fau, ca, bu'u ↔ fau: same time, place, or situation as…; ca: at the same time as… (present-like); bu'u: at a place (spatial analogue of ca).
nau ↔ At the speaker's time or place—useful in nested bridi so an inner tense can be anchored to the utterance (English-style sequence of tenses).
Modal terms and place tags together ↔ Modal terms do not remove fa–fu; both apply (e.g. mi klama se ka'a le rirxe le dinju still fills klama's places).
Several modal particles (imaginary journey) ↔ Read left to right from implied now/here; order scopes modals (e.g. mo'u co'a vs co'a mo'u); pu ba vs ba pu differs.
ce'e between modals ↔ Joins two modal particles at the same level so they are not nested, overriding the default left-to-right journey reading.
nu / du'u / ka ↔ Abstractors: event, proposition, property (-ness); closed with kei where applicable.
lu … li'u ↔ Quotation: Lojban text as a sumti; nestable.
zo ↔ Quotes the single following Lojban word.
sei ↔ Incidental bridi commenting on the host sentence.
ma / mo / xu ↔ Questions: sumti, selbri, yes/no respectively.
Attitudinals (interjections), placement ↔ Modify the construct immediately before them; at the start of a bridi they scope the whole bridi; moving them changes which phrase is in scope (learn-lojban lesson 1).
Attitudinal structure ↔ Root cmavo (e.g. ui, ie) plus optional scalar particles on the root; then optional suffixes pei, dai, zo'o; suffixes may take scalars too (e.g. ie zo'o nai = agree, not kidding).
Attitudinal scalars: cu'i, nai, sai, cai, ru'e ↔ cu'i = middle/neutral on the scale; nai = opposite pole (ui happy vs ui nai alas); sai = strong intensity; cai = extreme intensity; ru'e = weak intensity (standard Lojban scale; learn-lojban stresses cu'i, nai, sai).
pei (attitudinal suffix) ↔ Makes the attitudinal a question about the listener's feeling; pei alone asks for an appropriate attitudinal reply.
dai (attitudinal suffix) ↔ Attributes the feeling to someone else (empathy, e.g. ui nai dai = you must be sad); without dai the speaker expresses their own attitude toward the bridi.
zo'o / zo'o nai (attitudinal suffix) ↔ zo'o marks humor or non-serious tone; zo'o nai marks seriousness (I'm not joking).
Vocatives and attitudinal scalars ↔ Vocatives use the same scalar modifiers as interjections (e.g. ki'e sai = thank you very much).
Interjections before vocatives (sentence-wide scope) ↔ Put interjections before vocatives when both should modify the whole utterance; an interjection immediately after a vocative modifies that vocative or its argument (learn-lojban lesson 1).
ku / kei / vau ↔ Terminators: end LE description, NU abstraction, bridi (when required).
