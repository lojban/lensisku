## learn-lojban tutorial (notions; English Ōåö English, from lojban.pw course themes)

Alphabet Ōåö Latin letters; ' = /h/ between vowels; . = pause before vowel-initial words; stress on the second-to-last vowel.
selbrivla (brivla) Ōåö Relation/content word: consonant cluster within first five sounds, ends in a vowel (e.g. gleki, klama).
cmavo Ōåö Particle: consonant + vowel (+ optional 'V sequences); may be run together (e.g. lenu, naku).
cmevla Ōåö Name word: ends in a consonant; often written .name. with pauses matching dots.
Bridi Ōåö A clause: sumti fill numbered places xŌéü xŌéé ŌĆ” of one selbri (relation).
cu Ōåö Separates sumti cluster from selbri when needed; not used before nu/du'u/ka abstractions as selbri.
fa fe fi fo fu (place tags) Ōåö Tag sumti to selbri places xŌéüŌĆōxŌéģ; reorder sumti freely; unlike SE conversion they do not reshuffle the place structure; after be they mark places inside a tanru sumti (e.g. le klama be fi le tcadu).
Modal terms (tense, location, cause, etc.) Ōåö Apply to the whole bridi; they do not fill numbered selbri slotsŌĆöomitted slots are still zo'e unless a place tag assigns them (learn-lojban: ca, fa'a, pu, bu'u, ŌĆ”).
Modal term without a following sumti Ōåö Locates the event relative to the speaker's here and now (e.g. ba alone = future from now; bu'u alone = at this place).
Modal term with a following sumti Ōåö Locates the event relative to the event or state in that argument (e.g. ba le nu mi cadzu = after I walk).
Time and space (same principle) Ōåö Temporal distance uses pu/ba with zi/za/zu; spatial distance uses vi/va/vu (short / medium / far); vowel order iŌĆōaŌĆōu repeats for subjective near/medium/long.
la'u Ōåö Modal with a sumti stating how far in time or space (e.g. in three days).
fau, ca, bu'u Ōåö fau: same time, place, or situation asŌĆ”; ca: at the same time asŌĆ” (present-like); bu'u: at a place (spatial analogue of ca).
nau Ōåö At the speaker's time or placeŌĆöuseful in nested bridi so an inner tense can be anchored to the utterance (English-style sequence of tenses).
Modal terms and place tags together Ōåö Modal terms do not remove faŌĆōfu; both apply (e.g. mi klama se ka'a le rirxe le dinju still fills klama's places).
Several modal particles (imaginary journey) Ōåö Read left to right from implied now/here; order scopes modals (e.g. mo'u co'a vs co'a mo'u); pu ba vs ba pu differs.
ce'e between modals Ōåö Joins two modal particles at the same level so they are not nested, overriding the default left-to-right journey reading.
nu / du'u / ka Ōåö Abstractors: event, proposition, property (-ness); closed with kei where applicable.
lu ŌĆ” li'u Ōåö Quotation: Lojban text as a sumti; nestable.
zo Ōåö Quotes the single following Lojban word.
sei Ōåö Incidental bridi commenting on the host sentence.
ma / mo / xu Ōåö Questions: sumti, selbri, yes/no respectively.
ku / kei / vau Ōåö Terminators: end LE description, NU abstraction, bridi (when required).
